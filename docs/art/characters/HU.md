## 1. Backstory — the kid from 仙麓

Hu grew up in **仙麓 ("the Immortals' Foothill")** — the village at the base of 九仙山's pilgrim road — raised at his aunt's inn, the **奈何客棧**, after the road took his parents. The inn's name is the village's whole humor in two characters: *what can you do?* Pilgrims laugh at it on the way up. Nobody laughs at it on the way down.

仙麓 lives off the mountain: sells climbers straw sandals and courage going up, buys their gear back cheap when they come down done. **Hu grew up on what climbers left behind** — cracked scabbards, lucky charms, half a rack of returned weapons on the inn's back wall, and above all *stories*, told at the 奈何客棧's long table by people who had sparred with the Nine's disciples and come back changed in ways the boy only later learned to count.

He was never taken as a disciple. Schools want lineage or tuition; an inn boy has neither. So he learned the only way left: **watching through fences** — outer-gate drills over walls, guests re-enacting bouts after wine, one masked traveler who did something impossible in the courtyard and left before dawn. And he practiced with **whatever the buy-back wall had that week**: sword one month, staff the next, a spear with a cracked haft all winter. *(Diegetic reading: Hu is weapon-broad because the inn's wall was his armory — the base of the future weapon system, §5.)*

Twice he saved coin for a school's gate fee; twice he was laughed off before the inner door. At sixteen-and-change he did the thing every 仙麓 kid jokes about and no one does: packed his aunt's worst blanket, took a weapon off the wall, and **started climbing — to ask the Nine themselves.**

## 2. The mountain he finds (the warm act — and the wrongness underneath)

**The climb is not hostile — at first it is everything he dreamed.** The mountain is warm, courteous, *proper*: disciples bow before sparring, masters correct his footwork mid-bout and mean it kindly, shrines offer tea. Fights are **sparring matches with real stakes** — the jianghu's way of talking. Hu is welcomed the way a promising nobody is welcomed by a world that has plenty of room for one more student. He is being *taught*, and he is happy.

**The wrongness arrives as anomalies, not menace:** climbers he met at the 奈何客棧 never come down — or come down *abnormal*, smiling at nothing. A master mid-lesson says a sentence in the wrong order, twice, identically. The higher shrines are clean, too clean. The politeness never breaks; that becomes the horror.

**The real face is meta-gated:** only after Hu achieves what the surface story asks — defeating the false final boss(es) across runs / gathering the key relics *(exact gate designed in the narrative-structure slice)* — does the mountain show what it is. The within-run altitude bands (ART_DESIGN_DOC §6) stay; this meta-arc sits **above** them: early runs read warm even at altitude; the accumulated evidence — and the stamp book — reframe everything.

## 3. Motivation & arc

- **Surface want:** a master. Any of the Nine — he'd take the errand-boy job. He wants to belong to the thing he loves.
- **Under it:** proof that a nobody from the buy-back village can matter without a name.
- **The curdle:** the thing he loves is a feeding system. Every lesson he treasured was a spoonful.
- **The person he becomes:** the arc runs **curious → humorous → calm.** Early Hu fanboys mid-ambush and names techniques wrong with delight. Middle Hu jokes with an edge, because he's counting anomalies now. **Late Hu is quiet, precise, master-like** — the manner he worshipped, arrived at down a road none of the Nine took. The final inversion stands: from *"teach me"* to *"let go of everything they taught me"* — the buildless true fight is his life's thesis.

## 4. Personality & voice (per arc stage)

**Early (curious, humorous):**
- *"Wait — Crane school?? What's HE like??"* — mid-spar, to an opponent genuinely trying to win.
- *"That was Sleeve Wind! …I think. The left-handed one. Sorry about your ribs."* — names techniques out loud, slightly wrong; apologizes after winning.
- Counts stances under his breath; learned forms as pictures, never corrected.

**Middle (the counting):** the jokes continue but he's writing things in the stamp book that aren't autographs. *"You said that already. Word for word. …Anyway — again?"*

**Late (calm):** short sentences. Kindness without eagerness. He corrects a young disciple's footwork mid-bout and means it kindly — the full circle. *"Put it down. You can, you know."*

Not naive about people — inn kids read drunks and liars fine. It's the *legend* he couldn't see straight, and the legend is what breaks.

## 5. Weapons — the buy-back arsenal (system direction)

Hu practiced **many weapons** because the inn's wall dictated the curriculum. This grounds the future **weapon system**: each run, Hu takes **one weapon** from the wall; each weapon type has **variants**, plus **one hidden legendary variant** per type (Hades-aspect pattern). Long-term the wall grows toward the **十八般兵器** (the Eighteen Arms). **Chapter-1 scope: sword only** — the system ships later; the *fiction* ships now (the wall exists at the 奈何客棧; Hu's breadth is established).

**The chapter-1 sword — 贗劍, the replica:** a souvenir-row "Genuine Ninth Immortal Replica," proportions slightly off, the vendor's red tassel-tag still on the hilt. **Thematic load:** every authentic blade descends from the door's lineage — **the fake is the only clean sword on the mountain.** As Hu stains, stage by stage, the replica never does: an ink-veined hand on an untouched hilt. *(The hidden-legendary mystery hook for later: somewhere in the buy-back pile is a weapon that is real AND clean — and the reason why is a story.)*

## 6. Concept design

**Clothing — Chinese-authentic, Tang/Song commoner:** replace round-1's generic gear with historically grounded dress. Two directions for concept round 2 (user picks):
- **Song 短打 (working lean):** cross-collar short jacket (right over left), sash, loose trousers with leg wraps, cloth shoes — the athletic commoner cut, closest to "inn kid who trains."
- **Tang 圓領袍 (pilgrim neat):** round-collar robe hitched up for travel through the belt, soft cap or bare topknot — reads slightly more formal/pilgrim.
Both in the pilgrim blues (淵 湖 靄 + 雪 under-layer), **visibly self-mended** at sleeve and knee; no school colors or emblems; tiger strictly verbal.

**Base energy:** concept B's grin and light forward frame; **black hair**, short messy tail.
**Signature props:** the 贗劍 at the left hip (red tassel-tag) · the **stamp book 集印簿** at the right belt — a pilgrim's shrine-stamp book with shrine stamps, ticket stubs, two school refusal slips kept out of spite, and nine self-ruled empty slots. Its pages darken with the climb; in the middle arc it becomes his anomaly ledger. *(Future diegetic home for run records / boss marks / mirror moments.)*
**Cut:** charm clutter, bedroll/gourd/satchel — silhouette stays light, asymmetric (sword left hip, book right belt), unaffiliated with the six school stances.
**Expression sheet (per arc):** starstruck sparkle · eager grin · counting focus · post-parry astonishment · the ledger look (middle) · doubt · the mirror look · **the calm** (late — the master-like stillness).
**Maturation note:** late-arc Hu may carry visual maturation (posture, hair, the calm face) — ties to the gains-affect-appearance ambition; designed in the narrative-structure slice, not chapter-1 art.
